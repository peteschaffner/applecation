Linkinus 3

A concept for version 3 of the premier IRC client for OS X.

Primer

As you look through the mockups below, you will notice that I tried to imagine how a 2-pane Linkinus might work. I have always felt claustrophobic while using any 3-paned app (yes, this includes Chocolat). For those that feel the same, I thought it might be nice to offer users an option as to where the "User list" would be docked. I will stick to this layout for the remainder of these comps.

Groups

Groups are one of the features that sets your app apart, so I thought it would be best to make the creating/managing of them a key part of the UI.

The titlebar has been made thick, in order to accommodate buttons and other elements, and this is where the "Groups list" and "Group creator" buttons could live. The image below shows how the group manager, brought front and center in the UI, could live in a popup window. By taking group management out of the Preference pane, and into the main window, it will hopefully make for a smoother experience. The "Group creator", (or plus sign button) could be a quick way to slice up the chat view (I imagine holding the ⌥ modifier would insert a vertical split, rather than a horizontal). From here you could drag queries/channels in and build your perfect grouping.

Inline images

Another wonderful feature in Linkinus is the ability to view linked images inline. These next two mockups show how inline media might be treated… where "breaks" in the flow of messages would show glimpses of the linked image. Clicking on the area would unfold the full-sized image in all its beauty.

It would be great to see the same kind of functionality for video, and audio.

4-way split

Here we have a 4-way split, with one section ready to be populated.

In this particular mockup, the text input is stationary, as my gut tells me that positioning inputs relative to their respective chat section would be jarring for the user. I would, however, like to mockup an alternative, just to see what it would feel like, as I'm not fully convinced this is the right solution.

P.S. I am doing these designs on my MBA, thus the constrained window size ;)

Adding channels

The comp above illustrates a popover interface for discovering new channels based on a keyword search. I am not sure if their is a ranking system for the quality of IRC channels, but Nicholas mentioned ratings in the Kickoff task, so this accounts for that situation.

User list

This mockup shows an alternate user list that lives in a popover.

This concept needs a little more work, seeing as I would like to use popovers for the user info windows, and a popover off of another popover seems like bad news bears.
